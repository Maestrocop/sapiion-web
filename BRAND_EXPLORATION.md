## What a Good Name Needs

- **Memorable** — someone hears it once and remembers it
- **Pronounceable** — in Dutch and English
- **Distinctive** — not used in education software
- **Protectable** — strong enough as a trademark
- **Domain available** — .com or .education or .io preferred
- **Metaphorically relevant** — connects to education, intelligence, coordination or improvement
- **Short** — ideally 1–2 syllables, max 3

---

## Naming Styles to Explore

| Style | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Metaphor | Borrows meaning from another domain | Maestro, Compass, Atlas |
| Latin/Greek root | Classical, credible, international | Lumen, Gradus, Nexus |
| Invented/Coined | Unique, strong trademark potential | Elara, Velio, Nexio |
| Compound | Two concepts merged | EduPilot, LearnOps, CurrIQ |
| Abstract | No literal meaning, pure brand | Aria, Venn, Axon |

## Candidate Names

### Group 1 — Metaphor Names

---

#### MAESTRO ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Meaning:** The conductor who coordinates all elements of a performance

**Why it fits ILS:**
A maestro does not play an instrument. A maestro ensures that all players — curriculum, teachers, students, quality teams — perform together in harmony. That is exactly what ILS does.

**Strengths:**
- Strong, memorable metaphor
- Immediately understood in Dutch and English
- Signals coordination, mastery, excellence
- Works as: Maestro / Maestro EDU / Maestro Education

**Risks:**
- Common word — used across many industries
- Trademark check required in Benelux + EU (education software category)
- Several companies use "Maestro" in non-education sectors

**Domain status:** Check maestro.education / maestroedu.com / maestro.io

**Verdict:** Strong candidate. Trademark check required before committing.

---

#### COMPASS ⭐⭐⭐

## Strategic Naming Observation

**Avoid names containing:** AI, Smart, Learn, Edu, LMS, Tech, Digital

These words lock you into today's marketing category. They signal what you are *made of*, not what you *do*. They age badly and are weak trademarks.

ILS's vision is bigger than any of those words: **educational intelligence infrastructure.**

The best brand names do not describe the product — they create an identity that the product grows into.

- "Maestro" does not say "education software" — it says *coordination, mastery, excellence*
- "Meridian" does not say "analytics tool" — it says *the highest point, the reference line*
- "Lumira" does not say "reporting dashboard" — it says *light, vision, clarity*

Choose a name you will still be proud of in 10 years, when the product is bigger than you can imagine today.

## Trademark Check Resources

When you have your top 3–5 candidates, check these registers:

| Register | URL | Classes to check |
|---|---|---|
| Benelux (BOIP) | boip.int | Class 41 (education) + Class 42 (software) |
| EU (EUIPO) | euipo.europa.eu | Class 41 + Class 42 |
| International | wipo.int | If planning outside EU |

Also check: **Google** each name + "education software" / "edtech" to find unregistered but active brands.

## Recommended Next Steps

1. **Shortlist** — select top 3–5 candidates from summary table
2. **Domain check** — for each candidate: .com / .education / .io / .eu
3. **Trademark check** — Benelux (BOIP): boip.int / EU (EUIPO): euipo.europa.eu
   - Search in class 42 (software) and class 41 (education)
4. **Market search** — Google each name + "education software" / "edtech"
5. **Gut check** — say each name out loud in a sales meeting: "Welcome to [Name]"
6. **Decide** — one name, commit, move forward

## The Question to Ask Before Deciding

> "Can I say this name confidently in a meeting with a school director, and have them remember it the next day?"

If yes — it is a candidate.
If you hesitate — it is not.
