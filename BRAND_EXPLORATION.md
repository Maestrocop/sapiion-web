---

## What a Good Name Needs

- **Memorable** — someone hears it once and remembers it
- **Pronounceable** — in Dutch and English
- **Distinctive** — not used in education software
- **Protectable** — strong enough as a trademark
- **Domain available** — .com or .education or .io preferred
- **Metaphorically relevant** — connects to education, intelligence, coordination or improvement
- **Short** — ideally 1–2 syllables, max 3

---

## Naming Styles to Explore

| Style | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Metaphor | Borrows meaning from another domain | Maestro, Compass, Atlas |
| Latin/Greek root | Classical, credible, international | Lumen, Gradus, Nexus |
| Invented/Coined | Unique, strong trademark potential | Elara, Velio, Nexio |
| Compound | Two concepts merged | EduPilot, LearnOps, CurrIQ |
| Abstract | No literal meaning, pure brand | Aria, Venn, Axon |

## Candidate Names

### Group 1 — Metaphor Names

---

#### MAESTRO ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Meaning:** The conductor who coordinates all elements of a performance

**Why it fits ILS:**
A maestro does not play an instrument. A maestro ensures that all players — curriculum, teachers, students, quality teams — perform together in harmony. That is exactly what ILS does.

**Strengths:**
- Strong, memorable metaphor
- Immediately understood in Dutch and English
- Signals coordination, mastery, excellence
- Works as: Maestro / Maestro EDU / Maestro Education

**Risks:**
- Common word — used across many industries
- Trademark check required in Benelux + EU (education software category)
- Several companies use "Maestro" in non-education sectors

**Domain status:** Check maestro.education / maestroedu.com / maestro.io

**Verdict:** Strong candidate. Trademark check required before committing.

---

#### COMPASS ⭐⭐⭐
**Meaning:** Guides direction, helps navigate

**Why it fits ILS:**
ILS helps students, teachers and coordinators navigate the educational journey.

**Strengths:**
- Clear metaphor — guidance, direction, finding your way
- Short, memorable, international

**Risks:**
- Very common word — extremely high risk of trademark conflict
- Used by many companies in education and HR sectors

**Verdict:** Probably not available as a clean trademark. Low priority.

---

#### BEACON ⭐⭐⭐
**Meaning:** A signal that guides people to safety

**Why it fits ILS:**
Coordinators using ILS get early signals about at-risk students — like a beacon warning before the rocks.

**Strengths:**
- Strong metaphor for the early warning / intelligence angle
- Short and memorable

**Risks:**
- Used in some EdTech and HR platforms
- Less connected to curriculum/education specifically

**Verdict:** Possible. Worth checking availability.

---

#### POLARIS ⭐⭐⭐
**Meaning:** The North Star — the fixed point that guides navigation

**Why it fits ILS:**
ILS is the fixed reference point for an institution's educational quality.

**Strengths:**
- Strong, evocative, international
- Signals guidance and reliability
- Used in some sectors but not widely in education

**Risks:**
- Used by Polaris (vehicle manufacturer) — high brand recognition in other sectors
- Might require differentiation in branding

**Verdict:** Interesting. Check education software specifically.

### Group 2 — Latin / Greek Root Names

---

#### LUMEN ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Meaning:** Light (Latin) — clarity, illumination, insight

**Why it fits ILS:**
ILS brings clarity to education — for teachers, students, coordinators and institutions.

**Strengths:**
- Beautiful, simple, international
- Strong metaphor for intelligence and insight
- Widely understood across European languages

**Risks:**
- Used in some EdTech contexts (Lumen Learning in the US)
- Check EU trademark availability

**Verdict:** Strong candidate. Check Lumen Learning trademark scope — US-based, may not conflict in EU.

---

#### GRADUS ⭐⭐⭐
**Meaning:** Step, grade, level (Latin)

**Why it fits ILS:**
Learning is progression — step by step, grade by grade.

**Strengths:**
- Clear educational metaphor
- Distinctive, not widely used commercially
- International

**Risks:**
- Less immediately intuitive for non-Latin speakers
- Slightly academic feel — may appeal more to institutions than teachers

**Verdict:** Possible. Better as a sub-brand or feature name than a main brand.

---

#### NEXUS ⭐⭐⭐
**Meaning:** Connection, link, the point where things meet

**Why it fits ILS:**
ILS is the nexus of curriculum, teaching, assessment and student success.

**Strengths:**
- Directly describes ILS's value proposition
- International, professional

**Risks:**
- Very commonly used across technology companies
- Weak trademark potential

**Verdict:** Too common. Avoid.

---

#### MERIDIAN ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Meaning:** The highest point, the peak, the reference line

**Why it fits ILS:**
Meridian suggests reaching the highest point — educational excellence. Also a reference point — ILS as the single source of truth.

**Strengths:**

- Not widely used in education software
- Strong trademark potential

**Risks:**

- May feel too corporate for teachers and students

**Verdict:** Strong candidate for a premium positioning. Check trademark.

## Recommended Next Steps

1. **Shortlist** — select top 3–5 candidates from summary table
2. **Domain check** — for each candidate: .com / .education / .io / .eu
3. **Trademark check** — Benelux (BOIP): boip.int / EU (EUIPO): euipo.europa.eu
   - Search in class 42 (software) and class 41 (education)
4. **Market search** — Google each name + "education software" / "edtech"
5. **Gut check** — say each name out loud in a sales meeting: "Welcome to [Name]"
6. **Decide** — one name, commit, move forward

---

## The Question to Ask Before Deciding

> "Can I say this name confidently in a meeting with a school director, and have them remember it the next day?"

If yes — it is a candidate.
If you hesitate — it is not.
