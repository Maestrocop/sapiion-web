## Competitor 1 — Canvas (Instructure)

### What it is
The most widely used LMS in Dutch HBO. Strong in content delivery, assignment management and basic grading. Good UI. Well-known brand.

### Strengths
- Market leader — teachers already know it
- Strong content delivery and file management
- SpeedGrader for assignment marking
- LTI integrations (many third-party tools)
- Mobile app
- Large community and support ecosystem

### Weaknesses
- Content-centric, not outcome-centric
- No curriculum design — curriculum is managed outside Canvas
- No programme-level intelligence — coordinators are blind
- Outcome mapping exists but is shallow and rarely used in practice
- No student progression monitoring or BSA workflow
- No timetable or lesson planning
- Rubrics are basic — not connected to learning outcomes in a meaningful way
- Reports are limited — no real-time programme health

### Where ILS Wins
| Topic | Canvas | ILS |
|---|---|---|
| Curriculum design | Outside Canvas (Word/Excel) | Built-in Curriculum Composer |
| Outcome mapping | Manual, disconnected | Live, connected to assessment |
| Rubric assessment | Basic rubrics | Rubrics linked to outcomes and mastery |
| Programme monitoring | None | Real-time programme health dashboard |
| Student risk signals | None | Attendance + grade + outcome risk combined |
| Coordinator tools | None | BSA dashboard, progression reviews, advisor tools |
| Lesson planning | None | Built-in session and lesson planning |

### The Sales Conversation
> "Canvas is excellent for delivering content. ILS is built for something different — connecting curriculum intent to teaching to assessment to student success. Canvas doesn't ask whether learning happened. ILS does."

## Competitor 2 — Moodle

### What it is
Open-source LMS, widely used in education globally. Free to use but requires hosting and maintenance. Popular in budget-conscious institutions.

### Strengths
- Free / open source
- Highly customisable with plugins
- Large global community
- Flexible — can be shaped to many use cases

### Weaknesses
- Poor out-of-the-box UX — requires significant configuration
- Customisation creates maintenance burden
- No programme-level intelligence
- No curriculum design layer
- Outcome tracking is limited and rarely implemented correctly
- High implementation cost when hidden costs (hosting, development, support) are included
- No vendor support unless using MoodleCloud or a partner

### Where ILS Wins
| Topic | Moodle | ILS |
|---|---|---|
| Implementation | Complex, months of configuration | Structured 90-day onboarding |
| UX | Dated, requires training | Modern, role-specific interfaces |
| Programme intelligence | None | Built-in |
| True cost | Low license, high hidden costs | Transparent per-institution pricing |
| Support | Community / self-managed | Dedicated support |

### The Sales Conversation
> "Moodle is powerful but it requires significant investment to configure correctly. ILS is built specifically for Dutch professional education — the workflows, the roles, the BSA process, the outcome frameworks are already there. You don't configure ILS to fit education. ILS is built around it."

## Competitor 3 — Brightspace (D2L)

### What it is
Enterprise LMS with a strong focus on data and analytics. Used by larger institutions. More sophisticated than Canvas or Moodle in reporting.

### Strengths
- Strong analytics and reporting
- Good mobile experience
- Competency-based learning features
- Enterprise-grade security
- Strong SLA and support

### Weaknesses
- Expensive — enterprise pricing puts it out of reach for smaller institutions
- Analytics are reactive — reports on what happened, not what is happening
- No curriculum design layer
- No coordinator-specific tools for Dutch HBO workflows (BSA, SLB, progression reviews)
- Complex implementation — typically 12–18 months
- Interface is not intuitive for teachers

### Where ILS Wins
| Topic | Brightspace | ILS |
|---|---|---|
| Analytics | Historical reports | Real-time operational intelligence |
| Dutch HBO workflows | None | BSA, SLB, progression reviews built-in |
| Curriculum design | None | Built-in |
| Implementation time | 12–18 months | 90 days |
| Price | Enterprise (expensive) | Per-institution (accessible) |

### The Sales Conversation
> "Brightspace has analytics, but they show you what happened last month. ILS shows you what is happening now — which students need attention today, which outcomes are not being assessed, where the curriculum has gaps. And ILS is built specifically for Dutch professional education, not adapted from a global enterprise tool."

## Competitor 4 — Osiris (Topicus)

### What it is
The dominant Student Information System (SIS) in Dutch HBO. Manages official student records — enrolments, official grades, diplomas, BSA decisions. Almost every HBO institution uses Osiris.

### Strengths
- De facto standard in Dutch HBO
- Manages official student records and diplomas
- Deep integration with Dutch education regulation (DUO, BRON)
- Already implemented at most institutions

### Weaknesses
- Not a teaching tool — teachers do not use Osiris daily
- No curriculum design
- No learning outcomes connected to teaching
- No attendance tracking in a pedagogical sense
- No teacher-facing tools
- Reports are administrative, not educational

### Where ILS Wins
ILS does not replace Osiris — it works alongside it.

Osiris records the official result. ILS connects curriculum, teaching, assessment and the educational journey that leads to that result.

| Topic | Osiris | ILS |
|---|---|---|
| Official student records | Yes | No (ILS exports to Osiris) |
| Teaching and learning tools | No | Yes |
| Outcome-based assessment | No | Yes |
| Programme health monitoring | No | Yes |
| BSA preparation | Limited | Full workflow with evidence |

### The Sales Conversation
> "Osiris records decisions. ILS helps you make better decisions — and gives you the evidence to support them. They work together, not against each other."

## Competitor 5 — EduArte

### What it is
Dutch SIS and administration platform, popular in MBO. Manages student enrolment, attendance registration, and reporting for DUO.

### Strengths
- Built for Dutch MBO regulations
- Attendance registration for DUO reporting
- Student enrolment and administration
- Dutch language and support

### Weaknesses
- Administration tool, not an educational tool
- No curriculum design, no outcomes, no rubrics
- No teacher-facing educational tools
- No programme intelligence
- Limited learning analytics

### Where ILS Wins
Same positioning as Osiris — ILS adds the educational layer that EduArte does not have.

### The Sales Conversation
> "EduArte handles your administration. ILS handles your education. Together they give you a complete picture."

## Competitor 6 — Microsoft Teams + Office 365

### What it is
Not a competitor in the traditional sense — but in practice, many Dutch schools use Teams as a de facto LMS. Teachers share files, collect assignments and communicate through Teams.

### Strengths
- Already licensed — no additional cost
- Familiar to teachers and students
- Good communication and file sharing
- Integration with Microsoft ecosystem

### Weaknesses
- Not an educational tool — it is a communication tool used as one
- No curriculum structure
- No outcome-based assessment
- No rubrics or grading
- No programme intelligence
- No student progress tracking
- Creates enormous inconsistency — every teacher uses it differently

### Where ILS Wins
ILS is not a competitor to Teams — it is a complement.

Teams handles communication. ILS handles curriculum, teaching, assessment and intelligence.

ILS integrates with Microsoft 365 (and eventually Azure AD / SSO).

### The Sales Conversation
> "Teams is a great communication tool. But it was not built for education. ILS gives you the educational structure that Teams cannot — curriculum, outcomes, assessment, student success monitoring. They work together. ILS is not replacing Teams."

## The Meta-Competitive Argument

When a prospect compares ILS to any single competitor, they are thinking in the wrong category.

The real comparison is:

| Current situation | ILS |
|---|---|
| LMS + SIS + Excel + Email + Teams | One connected platform |
| 5 systems that don't talk to each other | One source of educational truth |
| Reactive — discover problems after they happen | Proactive — signals before problems escalate |
| Reports on the past | Intelligence about the present |
| Administration digitized | Education improved |

**ILS is not competing with one tool. ILS is replacing a landscape.**

## Frequently Asked Competitive Questions

**"We already have Canvas. Why would we add another system?"**
> "ILS is not adding to your landscape — it is replacing the gap Canvas leaves. Canvas delivers content. ILS connects curriculum to teaching to assessment to student success. If you have Canvas, you still have no programme intelligence, no coordinator tools, no curriculum composer. ILS fills that."

**"Moodle is free. Why would we pay for ILS?"**
> "Moodle's license is free. Implementation, hosting, configuration, maintenance and support are not. ILS is priced per institution with implementation and support included. Most institutions find the true cost of Moodle is higher than expected — and they still don't get the educational intelligence layer."

**"Osiris already does BSA."**
> "Osiris records the BSA decision. ILS prepares it — collects the evidence, monitors progression, flags risks early, documents the review. By the time you record the decision in Osiris, ILS has done the work."

**"We use Teams for everything."**
> "Teams is excellent for communication. ILS is for education. A teacher sharing a PDF in Teams and collecting assignments via a channel is not teaching — it is file management. ILS gives that teacher curriculum structure, rubric-based assessment, student progress visibility and programme-level intelligence. They are not the same thing."
