## VS LMS SECTION

**Section header:**
Sapiion is not a learning management system.

**Body:**
Canvas, Moodle and Brightspace are content delivery platforms. They were designed to distribute materials and collect submissions. That is useful — but it is not enough.

They do: **Content → Assignment → Grade**

Sapiion does: **Curriculum Intent → Learning Design → Teaching → Assessment Evidence → Mastery → Improvement → (repeat)**

The difference is fundamental. An LMS asks: *"Did the student submit?"*
Sapiion asks: *"Did the student develop the intended competency — and how do we know?"*

---

## VS SIS SECTION

**Section header:**
Sapiion is not a student administration system.

**Body:**
Osiris, Educator and similar SIS platforms manage student records — enrolments, credits, diplomas.

They record what happened. Sapiion helps improve what happens.

A SIS tells you a student failed. Sapiion tells you three weeks before the student fails — and gives the coordinator the tools to intervene.

---

## VS EXCEL SECTION

**Section header:**
Sapiion is not a better spreadsheet.

**Body:**
Most institutions today run on a combination of Canvas + Excel + email + separate attendance tools. This is the real competition.

The problem with the patchwork:
- Data lives in different places and never combines
- A coordinator cannot see grades, attendance and outcomes in one view
- A teacher rebuilds the same rubric every semester because there is no shared library
- A student checks three different places to understand their progress
- Accreditation requires weeks of manual evidence collection

Sapiion replaces the patchwork with one connected platform — and adds the intelligence that spreadsheets can never provide.

## THE ACCREDITATION ARGUMENT

**Section header:**
When accreditation comes, the evidence should already be there.

**Body:**
Dutch HBO institutions must demonstrate educational quality to the NVAO. That process is stressful and time-consuming for one reason: the evidence is scattered.

In Sapiion:
- Every learning outcome is linked to curriculum intent
- Every assessment is linked to rubric criteria
- Every rubric score is linked to outcomes
- Every student's progress is tracked against programme requirements
- Every programme's health is visible in real time

When accreditation comes, you review evidence — you do not collect it.
