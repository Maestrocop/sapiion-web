## What ILS Is

**ILS — Intelligent Learning System**

An Educational Intelligence Platform that connects curriculum design, teaching, assessment and student success into one continuous improvement cycle.

---

## Product Category

**Learning Operations Platform**

Not a next-generation LMS — that invites comparison with Canvas and Moodle on their terms.
Not a Learning Analytics Platform — that makes ILS sound like a reporting tool.

ILS is the **operating system for modern professional education**.

Just as:
- CRM manages customer operations
- ERP manages business operations
- ILS manages learning operations

## The Buyer

The person who signs the contract is typically:

- **Programme Manager** — responsible for educational quality and accreditation
- **Education Manager / Dean** — responsible for outcomes and student success
- **Department Head** — responsible for team performance and efficiency
- **Educational Innovation Manager** — looking for a better way to work
- **ICT Manager / CIO** — gatekeeper for security, integration and data

**The buyer is never the teacher. The buyer is never the student.**

The website and sales materials must speak to the buyer's concerns:
- Quality assurance and accreditation readiness
- Student retention and dropout reduction
- Operational efficiency
- GDPR and data security

### For the Institution
- Fragmented tools that don't communicate
- No single source of truth for curriculum and outcomes

### For Coordinators

- No programme-wide visibility
- Manual, time-consuming progress reviews

### For Teachers
- Administrative overload
- No connection between their grading and the curriculum intent
- No visibility into how students are doing across the programme

### For Students
- No clarity on where they stand
- Grades without insight
- No visibility into their own competency development

## What Makes ILS Different

### vs Canvas / Moodle / Brightspace
Those platforms are content delivery systems. They do Content → Assignment → Grade.

ILS does Intent → Design → Evidence → Mastery → Improvement.

ILS connects the entire educational lifecycle. They do not.

### vs Osiris / Educator (SIS systems)
Those systems manage student administration — enrolments, grades, diplomas.

ILS manages the educational process — curriculum, teaching, assessment, outcomes.

They record what happened. ILS helps improve what happens.

### vs Excel + email + separate tools
The current reality for most institutions.

ILS replaces the patchwork with one connected platform — and adds intelligence that spreadsheets can never provide.

## The Accreditation Argument

Dutch HBO institutions must demonstrate educational quality to the NVAO (Nederlands-Vlaamse Accreditatieorganisatie).

ILS makes that possible — not by generating reports at the end, but by maintaining evidence continuously:

- Every outcome linked to curriculum intent
- Every assessment linked to rubric criteria
- Every rubric score linked to learning outcomes
- Every student's progress tracked against programme requirements
- Every programme's health visible in real time

When accreditation comes, the evidence is already there.

---

## The Implementation Argument

Schools fear implementation risk more than they fear cost.

ILS addresses this directly:

- **Week 1–2:** Institution setup, user import, basic curriculum structure
- **Week 3–4:** Teacher onboarding, first classes created
- **Month 2:** First assessments, grading workflows live
- **Month 3:** Coordinator dashboards active, student progress visible
- **Month 6:** Full continuous improvement cycle running

ILS is not a 2-year implementation project. It is a structured 90-day onboarding.

## Pricing Philosophy

ILS is priced per institution — not per user, not per module.

**Why?**

Because educational quality is an institution-wide goal.
Limiting access by user count creates the wrong incentives — it discourages adoption by teachers and students.

Institutions pay for the platform. Everyone in the institution benefits.
